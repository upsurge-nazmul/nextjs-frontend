upsurge is India's first platform for educational games & gamified learning, allowing schoolchildren to understand entrepreneurship and financial literacy, and build modern skills. We have gamified the whole journey and are developing a suite of games that help our users learn in a fun, engaging and rewarding way.

We are looking to hire game developers with experience in designing hypercasual and simulator games in 2D and 3D, in Unity.

If you're interested in working on a truly new category within gaming and ed-tech, which will solve a critical problem for people and create immeasurable value, we look forward to speaking with you.

Responsibilities

  1. Work with the game development team and drive new games
  2. Work closely with the founders to create fun and effective learning games
  3. Define the best game experience (UX) for various target audience
  4. Analyze user feedback to modify and improve game design
  5. Design the game mechanics and user interface of each game
  6. Work with the platform team to develop and integrate APIs
  7. Convert storyboards into functional simulations and games (both 2D and 3D)

Requirements

  1. Passion for developing games that you are proud of
  2. At least 2 years post-college experience in game development
  3. Experience building Strategy and hypercasual games in WebGL & Mobile environments
  4. Ability to handle end to end game design and a demonstrable game design portfolio
  5. Understand game mechanics, gameplay fundamentals and use them to design good UX.
  6. A solid foundation in computer science, with strong competencies in data structures, algorithms, and software design.
  7. Experience managing projects using tools such as JIRA/Slack/Trello
  8. A team leader, who is a good communicator, sets an example for the whole team, and mentor the juniors to help them realize their potential

Compensation

We will be matching market salaries and will also include an equity component & share of product revenue. We have closed a seed round already, and compensation will not be a problem for the right candidate.
